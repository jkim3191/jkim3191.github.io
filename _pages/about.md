Hello👋, I'm **Jae Hyeon Kim**.

I am a **Robotics M.S. student** in the Mechanical Engineering Department at **Georgia Institute of Technology**.

I've worked on **robotics autonomy**, **navigation**, and **legged/humanoid locomotion**, with interests spanning optimization, physics-based modeling, and learning for robot decision-making and control.

I have particularly worked on developing models that improve the stability and robustness of bipedal locomotion on challenging terrain while reasoning about terrain uncertainty and the likelihood of failure during navigation. Looking ahead, I am excited by the broader challenge of developing robotic systems that can intelligently interact with people and operate autonomously in complex, human-centered environments. 

My interests extend beyond humanoids to a broad range of robotic platforms, including **manipulators**, **quadrupeds**, **AMRs**, **aerial robots**, and other **legged systems**. I am also interested in **teleoperation** and **VR-enabled robot control**.
