About Me

Hello 👋 and welcome to my website!

I am a Robotics M.S. student in the Mechanical Engineering department at Georgia Institute of Technology , where I conduct research on robotics at the LIDAR Lab .

My main interests are in locomotion, control, and navigation. I am especially interested in how optimization and physics-based modeling can be used to enable robust autonomy in complex, unstructured environments, including stability and path planning.

I have hands-on experience with ROS, simulation-based workflows, and hardware testing for developing, testing, and validating robots. Much of that work drew on physics-based simulation and model predictive and optimization-based control methods to generate dynamically feasible behaviors for legged and mobile robots under real-world constraints.

For me, choosing to pursue robotics research has been one of the most meaningful decisions I have made, and I am thoroughly enjoying the learning process and the challenges that come with it. 🙂

Through this website, I am gradually shaping my journey in robotics by sharing my experience, progress as my career progresses.
